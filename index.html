Danielle Eriksen Front-end Developer

<3's learning and cats, dreams of code

Initially I studied Front End Development with the hopes of simply rejuvenating my Graphic Design Diploma. I never anticipated how thoroughly I would get sucked into solving problems with my new-found coding skills. Since completing my studies and internship, I’ve enjoyed and struggled through everything from dev ops related errors, learning how to contribute to a team repo with git, discovered the ease of Ruby on Rails, delved into Rails MVC and fixed my own basic rspec tests.

I love sharing my knowledge with others as much as I enjoy expanding my own skill set, and have found tutoring young girls coding with Code4CT and introducing the Railsbridge course to newcomers extremely rewarding.

What else can I tell you about myself? I have an insatiable desire to learn (either on my own or under guidance), a strong and committed work ethic, no fear of working in new frameworks or new languages, a willingness to struggle through the unknown, a love of technology and the internet, and a passion for sharing what I’ve learnt with others.

I care about UX and making the web as accessible as possible. I am capable of creating custom designs, wire-frames and hi-fidelity mock-ups and translating them into a front-end ui and love solving interactivity puzzles with javaScript.
